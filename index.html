WHO AM I?

My name is Roshaun Brady. I am studying Mechanical Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. I will be graduating May 2020 and am currently looking for full time positions. I would love to work on electromechanical applications, such as modeling control systems, and test engineering for hardware or mechanical product design. Over time, I have become proficient in using Python, C, Arduino, MySQL, Tableau, PSpice, NX Cad, and Adobe Illustrator.

Most recently, I worked at Tesla as a Hardware Engineering Intern for their Compliance Department. There, I analyzed the behavior of the Megapack, V3 solar panels, and MCI's, further developed code for data acquisition and anlysis, and created reliability test documentation for various in-house products.

I am curious about my world and determined to be a part of creative solutions that ultimately give me a sense of purpose. This is why I move through life with intention. I hope that you find something interesting as you go through the site! Thanks for stopping by.

PROJECTS

Processing Game

Developed a visual game experience as a final project for my Computer Processing/Vision course. The purpose of this interactive game was to evoke three different experiences and emotions from the viewer. It is comprised of three rooms and a main room which served as a starting point and instructionary guide. Each room was an object-rendered 3D space and movement was developed using a unit circle approach and vector math. This game was developed using Processing Application, and Java.

JAVA, PROCESSING, ADOBE ILLUSTRATOR

Smart Car

Enhanced a smart car for my Embedded Controls Course using C and through hardware implementation. Throughout the course, I worked on a team of 3 to meet different car developments set by the course. I primarily focused on coding for this course and would alternate between hardware and testing depending on my partners' involvement. For one project, we were able to get the car to detect obstacles such as a wall or a book and turn to avoid collision using a ranger. In another project, we used an accelerometer to guage when the car reached level ground after climbing up or down an incline. For the final project, we shiftted our experience towards a small gondola, where we played with hardware components to affect the speed of the system and halt motion at different angles or in response to an approaching obstacle, respectively.

C, SiLabs IDE APPLICATION (C8051), CIRCUITRY/HARDWARE IMPLEMENTATION

Asasin Software

Developed image anlyzing software using OpenCv and Python for patented micro-LED stages. Main concepts used were thresholding and line detection to identify magnets. Data collected from image were used to calculate the accuracy of completed arrays by detemining the centroid of each LED and comparing the pitch distance to its desired value. Appropriately titled 'The Amazing Self Array Supreme Intelligent Nexus Software'.

OPENCV, PYTHON
